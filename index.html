1
Day
June 19 · Thursday
Arrival & Check-in to Paradise
Jammu to Srinagar · The Scenic Mountain Drive
Pickup from Station ~6–7 hr drive
  • 🚐
    Pickup from Jammu Railway Station — Your tour operator's cab/tempo-traveller will greet you at the platform. Freshen up quickly!
  • 🛣️
    NH 44 — The Jammu-Srinagar Highway — Drive through the breathtaking Banihal Pass (altitude ~2,200m), the famous Jawahar Tunnel (2.85 km), and the lush Jhelum valley. Patnitop stopover recommended for tea and views.
  • 🏔️
    Patnitop — A beautiful hilltop meadow at 2,024m. Enjoy a short walk, mountain air, and Kashmiri noon chai (pink salted tea with bread).
  • 🏨
    Arrive Srinagar & Check-in — Settle into your hotel. Evening at leisure — stroll along Boulevard Road beside the shimmering Dal Lake. Watch the shikara boats glide in the sunset light.
🌸 Tonight: Try Wazwan (traditional Kashmiri multi-course feast) at your hotel or a local restaurant. Don't miss Rogan Josh and Gushtaba!
2
Day
June 20 · Friday
Sonamarg — Gold Meadow of the Himalayas
Day excursion • ~87 km from Srinagar
Altitude 2,740m Return to Srinagar
  • ⏰
    Early departure ~7:00 AM — Beat the tourist rush. The drive follows the Sind River gorge through pine forests and alpine meadows — magical in the morning mist.
  • 🏔️
    Thajiwas Glacier — The highlight of Sonamarg! Hire a pony or trek (~3 km) to this ancient glacier. Touch real glacier ice, photograph the turquoise glacial stream. A surreal experience at any age.
  • 🌊
    Nilagrad River — On the way back, stop at this red mineral water stream — famous for its supposedly medicinal waters. Locals swear by a sip!
  • 🌅
    Return to Srinagar by evening — Relax on your houseboat balcony if staying on Dal Lake. Watch the famous Srinagar sunset paint the water gold.
❄️ What to wear: June is warm in Srinagar (18–25°C) but Sonamarg can be 8–14°C. Carry a warm jacket, sunscreen, and sunglasses — glacier UV is intense!
3
Day
June 21 · Saturday
Gulmarg — Meadow of Flowers
World's highest gondola ride • ~51 km from Srinagar
Altitude 2,650m Gondola to 4,200m!
  • 🚡
    Gulmarg Gondola (Phase I & II) — One of the world's highest and longest cable car systems. Phase I reaches Kongdoori (3,100m) — views of Nanga Parbat (8,126m)! Phase II goes to Apharwat Peak (4,200m) — you'll see snow in June. Book tickets online to avoid queues.
  • ⛷️
    Apharwat Peak (if fit!) — At 4,200m, you're above the treeline in a lunar snow landscape. June means large snowfields — you can walk/play in snow. Altitude caution: take it slow, hydrate well.
  • 🌺
    Gulmarg Meadow Walk — The bowl-shaped meadow is carpeted with wildflowers in June — buttercups, daisies, wild roses. Golf course here is one of the world's highest! Take a pony ride around the meadow.
  • 🕌
    St. Mary's Church & Maharani Temple — Colonial-era stone church and hilltop temple with panoramic views before heading back to Srinagar.
📷 Photo ops: The view of Mt. Nanga Parbat from the gondola on a clear day is life-changing. Early morning departures get the clearest skies.
4
Day
June 22 · Sunday
Pahalgam — Valley of Shepherds
Betaab Valley · Aru Valley · Baisaran • ~96 km
Altitude 2,130m Lidder River
  • 🌊
    Betaab Valley — Named after the 1983 Bollywood classic filmed here. Crystal-clear Lidder River rushing through deodar forests with snow-capped peaks — arguably Kashmir's most photogenic valley. Perfectly walkable.
  • 🐄
    Aru Valley — A serene village 12 km from Pahalgam, accessible only by local jeep. Traditional Bakarwal shepherd huts, yaks grazing, wildflower meadows. Less touristy — more authentic.
  • 🏕️
    Baisaran (Mini Switzerland) — Pine-fringed alpine meadow accessible only by pony/foot. 360° mountain views, no vehicles. Pure bliss. Many say it's their single favourite spot in all of Kashmir.
  • 🎣
    Trout Fishing on Lidder — Pahalgam is famous for brown trout. Try licensed fishing for an hour — many operators offer gear rental along the river bank.
🕌 Note: June is peak Amarnath Yatra season — Pahalgam is the base camp. Expect crowds on the main road but side valleys remain peaceful. Book local jeeps in advance.
5
Day
June 23 · Monday
Srinagar — The Jewel City
Dal Lake · Mughal Gardens · Old City & Craft Bazaars
Shikara Ride Local Sightseeing
  • 🛶
    Shikara Ride on Dal Lake (Sunrise) — Hire a shikara at dawn (~₹1,200–1,500/hr). Glide through lotus beds, floating vegetable markets, and past ornate houseboats as the city stirs to life. Truly magical.
  • 🌳
    Mughal Gardens — Visit Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashme Shahi — UNESCO-listed terraced gardens built by Mughal emperors along the Dal lakefront. Chinar trees, fountains, and mountain backdrops.
  • 🕌
    Jama Masjid & Old City — The grand 14th-century mosque with 378 wooden pillars. Wander the narrow lanes of the old city (Nowhatta, Maharaj Gunj) for authentic Kashmiri crafts.
  • 🛍️
    Shopping! — Buy pashmina shawls, hand-knotted carpets, walnut wood carvings, saffron, Kashmiri kahwa tea, paper mache boxes. Go to Lal Chowk and Polo View market. Bargain warmly but fairly.
🍵 Must try today: Harisa (slow-cooked meat porridge, available only in mornings), Sheermal (saffron flatbread), and Kehwa tea at a traditional Srinagar café.
6
Day
June 24 · Tuesday
Farewell Kashmir · Drive to Jammu
Check-out & return journey southward
Check-out by 10 AM ~6 hr drive
  • 🌅
    Last morning on Dal Lake — Watch the dawn from your balcony. A quiet cup of kahwa and a final sunrise over the water — memories to last a lifetime.
  • 🚐
    Drive back to Jammu Tawi — Reverse the scenic NH-44. Stop at Banihal or Patnitop for lunch. Arrive Jammu by early evening.
  • 🛕
    Vaishno Devi Darshan (optional) — If energy permits, the famous Mata Vaishno Devi shrine is 40 km from Jammu. Helicopter service (Katra to Sanjichhatt) makes it manageable.
  • 🏨
    Overnight in Jammu — Rest at a hotel near Jammu Tawi station. Explore Raghunath Temple and the famous Gulab Bhavan market.

Phase 3 — Your Free Days

June 25–26 · Explore Punjab, Delhi or Agra — your call!

🏯 Delhi — City of Empires
~590 km from Jammu · Direct trains
  • Red Fort & Chandni Chowk breakfast
  • Qutub Minar & Humayun's Tomb
  • India Gate evening walk
  • Lodhi Art District (street art + coffee)
  • Connaught Place & Janpath shopping
  • Metro ride across the capital
🕌 Agra — The Taj & Beyond
~2 hrs from Delhi by Shatabdi
  • Taj Mahal at dawn (most magical time)
  • Agra Fort — Mughal grandeur
  • Mehtab Bagh — Taj from the other side
  • Fatehpur Sikri (40 km day trip)
  • Agra petha (sweet) and leather shopping
🌾 Punjab — Heart & Soul
Amritsar is 6 hrs from Jammu
  • Golden Temple — most serene place on Earth
  • Wagah Border Retreat Ceremony (sunset)
  • Jallianwala Bagh memorial
  • Amritsari kulcha & lassi breakfast
  • Lawrence Road street food tour
  • Partition Museum (powerful & moving)
✈️ Suggestion: June 25 — Jammu to Amritsar by train (Jammu-Amritsar Express, ~5–6 hrs). June 26 — Amritsar to Delhi, overnight journey/sleeper, reach Delhi morning of June 27 in time for your return train from NZM.

Essential Packing List

June in Kashmir — warm days, cold nights, and everywhere in between

Clothing

  • Warm jacket / fleece (for Sonamarg/Gulmarg)
  • Light casuals for Srinagar city
  • Trekking shoes + sandals
  • Rain poncho / light waterproof
  • Sunglasses & sun hat
  • Warm socks (2–3 pairs)

Documents & Finance

  • Original Aadhaar / Voter ID
  • All train tickets (printout + digital)
  • Tour operator contact + voucher
  • Cash in multiple small denominations
  • UPI apps (Paytm, PhonePe)
  • Emergency contacts written down

Health & Safety

  • High SPF sunscreen (SPF 50+)
  • Altitude sickness tablets (Diamox)
  • Lip balm & moisturiser
  • Basic first aid kit
  • Any personal medications
  • Electrolyte sachets

Train Essentials

  • Power bank (20,000 mAh)
  • Ear plugs & eye mask
  • Travel pillow / inflatable
  • Dry snacks & biscuits
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Small padlock for luggage
